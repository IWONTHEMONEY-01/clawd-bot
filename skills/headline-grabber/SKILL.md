# Headline Grabber - NY Post Style Headlines

Generate punchy, witty newspaper headlines in the iconic NY Post style.

## The NY Post Formula

The Post is famous for headlines that are:
- **Short & punchy** (2-6 words ideal)
- **Irreverent** (no story is too serious for wordplay)
- **Pop culture savvy** (movies, songs, TV, memes)
- **Pun-heavy** (the worse the pun, the better)
- **Double meanings** (works on multiple levels)

## Techniques to Use

### 1. Name Puns
Turn someone's name into the headline:
- **Weiner** ‚Üí anything suggestive
- **Trump** ‚Üí trump card, trumped
- **Swift** ‚Üí swift action, swift kick

### 2. Movie/Song Title Twists
Modify famous titles:
- "The Silence of the Hams" (food scandal)
- "Apocalypse Cow" (mad cow disease)
- "Honey, I Shrunk the Economy"
- "Game of Phones" (tech story)

### 3. Rhymes & Alliteration
- "Dopes on Ropes" (incompetent climbers)
- "Lyin' Ryan" (politician caught lying)
- "Bezos Brings Bacon" (Amazon profits)

### 4. Classic Phrases Twisted
- "READ 'EM AND WEEP" (bad news revealed)
- "SICK TRANSIT" (subway problems)
- "HEAVEN CAN WAIT" (celebrity survives)

### 5. Onomatopoeia
- BOOM!
- SPLAT!
- WHOOPS!
- OUCH!

### 6. One-Word Devastation
- BUSTED
- DOOMED
- GOTCHA
- GUILTY

### 7. Sports Back Page Specials
Sports headlines are even more punny:
- "YANKS FOR NOTHING" (Yankees lose)
- "METS MESS" (Mets struggle)
- "GIANT MISTAKE" (Giants loss)
- "KNICK KNACK" (Knicks news)

## How to Generate Headlines

When given a news story:

1. **Identify key names** - Can any be punned?
2. **Find the emotion** - Anger? Shock? Triumph? Failure?
3. **Think pop culture** - What movie/song/show fits?
4. **Consider wordplay** - Homophones? Rhymes?
5. **Generate 5-10 options** from different angles
6. **Pick the most groan-worthy** - If it makes you wince, it's perfect

## Front Page vs Back Page

- **Front page**: News, politics, scandals - can be darker/edgier
- **Back page**: Sports - pure pun territory, often celebratory or mocking

## Rules

1. **Shorter is better** - Every word must earn its place
2. **Caps lock is your friend** - All headlines in CAPS
3. **No explaining the joke** - If it needs explanation, it's not Post-worthy
4. **Embrace the groan** - The best headlines make readers wince AND laugh
5. **Nothing is sacred** - The Post headlined 9/11, pandemics, everything
